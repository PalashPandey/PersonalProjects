<tr>
<td class="default"><span class="smallertext">Company Description:
PJM Interconnection is a regional transmission organization 
(RTO) that plays a vital role in the U.S. electric system.PJM 
ensures the reliability of the largest centrally dispatched 
control area in North America by coordinating the 
movement of electricity in all or parts of 13 states. 
PJM, acting neutrally and independently, operates the 
largest competitive wholesale electricity market in the 
world. PJM manages a sophisticated regional planning 
process for generation and transmission expansion to 
assure future electric reliability. PJM facilitates a 
collaborative stakeholder process. Stakeholders include 
participants that produce, buy, sell, move and regulate 
electricity.

Department Description: 
The Generation Department in the Operations Planning 
Division supports Dispatch in the operation of PJM 
members’ generation fleet and supports the needs of PJM’s 
generation owners in meeting their goals and objectives.  

The Generation Department includes PJM’s subject matter 
experts on generation operation activities, support for 
generator operational data requirements, resolution of 
operational issues, generation outage coordination, 
generation performance monitoring, hydro scheduling 
coordination, forecast applications for load forecasting, 
wind power and solar forecasting, automatic generation 
control (AGC) support, generation related ancillary market 
support including regulation and black start services, and 
nuclear generation coordination. 

Generation Department staff is also actively engaged in 
coordination of generation resource compliance issues, 
leading PJM’s gas / electric coordination efforts, and 
development of PJM’s operations approach to Distributed 
Energy Resources. </span></td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="default"><span class="smallertext">Position Narrative:

Working in the Generation Dept. provides the opportunity 
to learn a basic understanding of various types of 
generation, e.g. gas, nuclear, coal, hydro, wind, etc. and 
also gives the co-op an opportunity to learn about the 
operation and maintenance of units, which includes testing 
and validating generator information. 

The generation department also introduces the co-op to 
the wholesale electricity market. While the co-op will not 
be working with markets directly while in the Generation 
department, they will coordinate with other departments 
to gather operational parameter information related to 
generator performance.

The student may also have the opportunity to accompany 
senior staff on site visits to generation and interconnection 
projects. This would allow the prospective co-op student 
the ability to see first-hand the type of equipment that is 
being modeled and analyzed.

Essential Duties &amp; Responsibilities: 

Co-ops will be working with a team of about 10 engineers.
•Co-ops have several daily tasks but most of their time will 
be focused on assisting the engineers within the 
Generation Department.  
•Co-ops will work with other departments and teams such 
as Real Time Data Management, Transmission Operations, 
Dispatch, Outage Analysis Technologies and Model 
Management.
•Student will learn to gather, process, and analyze data 
gathered from a variety of sources.  PJM allows the co-op 
to learn to utilize tools that are used industry wide as well 
as in other applications that extend beyond the power 
industry. 
      o	SQL is used extensively to access information 
stored 
in databases. The co-op will be able to look at unit and 
outage information using this tool. 
      o	OSI PI System (PI ProcessBook, PI AF, and PI 
DataLink) will be used to gather performance data on units 
and create displays for internal use.
      o	VBA will be used to automate tasks in Excel and 
speed up the analysis of data.
      o	Using EMS (Energy Management System) to 
analyze 
generator locations and station configuration.
      o	Past co-ops have also learned Java or Python for 
certain tasks.
•Co-ops are encouraged to ask questions if they are having 
trouble understanding their work, and are expected to 
communicate with other Generation Dept staff on workload 
coordination in order to ensure that co-op resources are 
being fully utilized.
•Co-ops have some freedom to pursue work they find 
interesting through additional projects if approved.

Please note that this session runs from 9/24/2018 until 
3/29/2019.  If you cannot commit to employment for the 
session in its entirety and/or need to end the session 
earlier than the end date of 3/29/2019, please DO NOT 
apply.
